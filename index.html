Microsoft Design Language

Metro UI was Windows reimagined as a fast, tile-based information surface.

First introduced on Windows Phone and later pushed into Windows 8, Metro UI emphasized bold typography, flat color, motion, and content-first layouts. Its signature Live Tiles were designed to surface useful information at a glance instead of burying it behind icons and menus.

Why It Still Matters

I still love Metro UI and genuinely miss how clear and alive it felt.

Metro had a confidence that most modern interfaces still do not match. The large type, bright tiles, sharp layout, and live information made the system feel personal, immediate, and optimistic. It felt like software with a point of view, and that is exactly why I still miss it.
